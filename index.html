Nathaniel Houk

In 2015 I founded Epsilon Records a boutique record label featuring Techno, Acid and Deep House. We livestream on Twitch and are actively signing new artists!

I did my bachelors at the University of Southern California where I graduated in 2008 summa cum laude with a 4.0 major GPA in Computer Engineering and Computer Science. I worked as a teaching assistant for EE106 taught by Professor Mark Redekopp.

In Fall 2005 I won the USC Programming Contest writing my solutions in C++. My EE459 senior design project involved writing embedded C and reverse engineering the Apple 30-pin connector to build an alarm clock called iSnooze.

In 2006 I founded the USC Chapter of Engineers Without Borders and served as the President through 2008. The EWB-USC club is a 501(c)3 organization dedicated to improving lives through sustainable water and related engineering projects.

I grew up on Bainbridge Island, WA and graduated from Bainbridge High School in 2004.

Title: Reconciling the MAD Paradox with P vs NP within M-Theory's Framework: Computational and Theoretical Physics Implications
Authors: NJH, RWU, SBF, RCH, SLP, DPD
Affiliations: National Security Agency, United States; Thomas Lord Department of Computer Science, University of Southern California; Clay Mathematics Institute

Abstract: This study introduces an innovative approach to the P=NP problem, framed within M-theory, and explores the ramifications of the Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD) paradox for computational complexity and theoretical physics. By analyzing how P=NP could be considered equivalent to, or different from, the MAD condition, we offer a theoretical model that harmonizes these ideas using Gödel's incompleteness theorems and the concept of a multiverse.

Citations: pequalsnp.com registered in 2008 by the late Dutch Professor Dr. Woegi of Eindhoven University of Technology who passed in 2022, sadly unaware of the 2015 resolution to P equals NP Clay Millennium problem (link)
Original Publication: pequalsnp.org registered in May 2015 by NJH.

Fields Metal: The initial ideas that form the basis of this research were first recorded and verified publicly via a blockchain proof of existence on May 30, 2015 vital for the proper acknowledgment of computer scientist deserving the Fields Medal for their contributions due to the multiple competing academic groups in 2016 working on P vs NP resolution.
Time Proof: An immutable record of the proof was made on the blockchain in May 2015, available at proofofexistence.com

Support: This research was funded by the NSA Mathematical Sciences Program and a 2012 NSF grant.
Submission Details: Submitted in July 2015 for review, receiving endorsement from NSA MSP referees in 2018, and under a top-secret classification embargo order until 2032.
